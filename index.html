Publications

Real-time detection of Lung cancer using CNN

Published in:
2023 2nd International Conference on Vision Towards Emerging Trends in Communication and Networking Technologies (ViTECoN)

Date of Conference: 05-06 May 2023

  • In pioneering research, I spearheaded the development of a real-time lung cancer detection system using Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs), addressing the global challenge of lung cancer, the second most prevalent cancer. Focusing on chest CT imaging, we aimed to mitigate the reliance on radiologist expertise by creating a Computer-Aided Detection (CAD) solution. Our method involves meticulous preprocessing of digitized chest CT images before CNN input, trained on an extensive dataset. Recent successes include a CAD system achieving an AUC of 0.94 in identifying lung nodules, affirming CNNs' high accuracy in lung cancer detection. Our proposed system's real-time analysis capability holds profound implications for clinical settings, potentially aiding radiologists in managing image volumes and improving patient outcomes. Optimized through preprocessing steps, the CNN exhibited remarkable performance, achieving 91.18% accuracy and 0.25% sensitivity on a dataset of 2000 chest CT images. This research highlights deep learning's transformative potential in enhancing lung cancer diagnosis efficiency and accuracy, positioning the technology as a valuable asset in clinical settings. Future avenues may explore additional imaging techniques and larger datasets for further validation and enhancement.
